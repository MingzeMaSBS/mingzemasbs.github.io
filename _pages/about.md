I am a 3rd-year PhD student in Finance at Saïd Business School, University of Oxford. I hold a master’s degree in economics from Kyoto University and a bachelor’s degree in accounting from Tongji University.

My current research centers on idea diffusion, technology adoption, and innovation. I also have broader interests in trade theory, markups, and resource misallocation. I am particularly drawn to the mathematical elegance of economic models built around extreme value distributions and gamma functions. Before starting my PhD, I worked on corporate governance.
